# Memoria — Thesis Writing Coordination

This folder holds supporting materials for the thesis **Plataforma de Evaluación y Recomendación de modelos de Demanda (PRED)**.

## Workflow Overview

The primary writing home is **Overleaf**, where the team co-edits the official template (`Final Thesis structure.tex`) in real time. The Overleaf document is the authoritative live version managed by the project captain.

This folder serves three functions:

1. **Synced Exports**: Compiled versions of chapters/sections are exported from Overleaf and versioned here as backup and reference, organized by chapter or sprint.

2. **Bitácora (Development Log)**: `bitacora-vi.md` is the rolling event log that feeds Chapter VI (Desarrollo de la Solución). Throughout each sprint, the team logs notable events — design decisions, specification corrections, plan deviations, and reference findings — so that Chapter VI can be assembled cohesively in the final integration phase.

3. **Source Notes**: Chapter owners maintain working notes and references here during drafting, organized by chapter. Once integrated into the Overleaf master template, notes may be archived or consolidated.

## Chapter Ownership

Chapter VI (Desarrollo de la Solución) is written by the whole team: each member drafts the subsections for the sprints and layers they led; one member assembles the chapter from the bitácora and team contributions; the editor's style pass ensures one consistent voice. Other chapters are individually owned by specific team members. The global editor's style pass ensures coherence across all 59 pages.

## Structure

- `bitacora-vi.md` — rolling log feeding Chapter VI; entries appended throughout development
- `{chapter-id}/` — source notes organized by chapter (optional; use as needed)
- Other chapter exports as they are finalized
